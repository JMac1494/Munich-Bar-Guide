Beer Halls
The soul of Munich. Long communal tables, brass bands, and steins the size of your head. Start here.
Hofbräuhaus München
Platzl 9, Altstadt · +49 89 290136100

Beer Hall
The Vibe
The world's most famous beer hall, founded in 1589. Cavernous, raucous, and unapologetically touristy — but entirely necessary on a stag. Seats 1,300+ inside, plus another 1,000 in the beer garden. Long communal tables perfect for a group. Oompah brass band plays nightly in the main hall.

Biergarten
The Vibe
5,000 seats under 100+ chestnut trees — Munich's third largest beer garden. Founded 1812. The beer comes straight from wooden barrels — a bell rings every time a new barrel is tapped. Mix of locals and visitors, warm and lively without being overwhelmingly touristy. Has a cosy underground storage cellar for rainy days.

Löwenbräukeller
Nymphenburger Str. 2, Maxvorstadt · +49 89 526021
loewenbraeukeller.com
Exceptional for large groups — purpose-built event hall up to 2,000
Beer Hall
The Vibe
A Munich institution since 1883 — the grandest beer hall most tourists miss. Magnificent Bavarian architecture right on Stiglmaierplatz, with a huge banquet hall, a warren of smaller rooms, and a classic shaded chestnut beer garden for summer. Fewer tourists than the Altstadt halls, livelier local crowd, and steeped in proper Munich history. Live music adds to the already festive atmosphere most evenings.
Beers on Offer
Löwenbräu Original (Helles) Löwenbräu Dunkel Löwenbräu Weissbier Kellerbier Dunkel Seasonal Oktoberfestbier
Entertainment
🎺 Live music on selected evenings — traditional Bavarian band in the main hall. The grand banquet hall has hosted everything from Bavarian political satire to massive group celebrations for over a century.
Opening Hours
Daily: 10am – Midnight
Beer garden also open in good weather
Large Group Notes
✓ Rooms available for 10–2,000 people ✓ Private room options for groups ✓ Reservations actively welcomed ✓ Event configurator on website
Why Go Here
Genuinely one of the most impressive beer hall buildings in Munich — and because it sits just outside the tourist centre at Stiglmaierplatz, the crowd is almost entirely local. You drink Löwenbräu at its freshest, right at the brewery's historic home.
Best for: Saturday evening before heading home — or as your Friday warm-up before the Altstadt crawl. Book a room for the group and treat it as your base for the night.
Paulaner Bräuhaus
Kapuzinerplatz 5, Ludwigsvorstadt · +49 89 5446110
paulaner-brauhaus-muenchen.com
Good for groups — book a table, especially in summer
Brewery Tap
The Vibe
The flagship tap for one of Munich's most famous breweries — and uniquely, they brew on-site, with gleaming copper kettles visible from the bar. Stunning architecture inside: huge columns, soaring arched windows, long communal tables, and an open kitchen. A large summer beer garden outside. Calmer and more neighbourhood-feeling than the tourist-heavy Altstadt halls — locals genuinely come here.
Beers on Offer
Paulaner Helles (house) Paulaner Weissbier Zwicklbier (unfiltered) Rotbier (amber) Salvator (dark bock) Radler
Unique Feature
🍺 Beer brewed on the premises — the brewhouse is right by the bar. You can watch the brewmaster at work while you drink the results. One of very few places in Munich where the beer has genuinely never travelled.
Opening Hours
Daily: 11am – Midnight
Large Group Notes
✓ Multi-floor venue — plenty of space ⚠ Book ahead for groups of 15+ ✓ Large beer garden for summer ✓ English-friendly staff
Food Note
The Schweinshaxe, Zwickl-braised beef goulash, and sausage pans are outstanding. Larger portions than most — worth eating here rather than just drinking through.
Best for: A proper lunch session on Day 1 or 2 — watch the brewer, drink the result, eat something large, feel like a king of Bavaria.
Weisses Bräuhaus im Tal
Tal 7, Altstadt · 3-min walk from Marienplatz · +49 89 2901380
weisses-brauhaus.de
Large groups welcome — easy to walk in at midday
Wheat Beer Hall
The Vibe
The home of Schneider Weisse — Germany's oldest wheat beer brewery and widely considered its finest. A Munich institution since 1872, with rustic wooden beams, long communal tables, a lively buzz, and all the Bavarian character you'd want. Favoured by locals and visiting beer enthusiasts alike. Located right in the Altstadt, extremely easy to combine with Hofbräuhaus or Tegernseer Tal on a crawl.
Beers on Offer
Schneider Weisse Original Schneider Weisse Tap 7 (flagship) Aventinus (strong wheat doppelbock) Schneider Weisse Tap 1 (light) Lovebeer (passion fruit & lime) Seasonal specials
Why it's Different
🌾 Entirely wheat beer focused — the best range of Weissbier you'll find anywhere. The Aventinus (dark wheat doppelbock at 8.2%) is a legendary beer. If the group hasn't tried proper Weissbier, this is where to do it.
Opening Hours
Daily: 10am – Midnight
Mains approx. €14–26
Large Group Notes
✓ Communal tables — built for sharing ✓ Groups easily accommodated at lunch ⚠ Evenings get busy — book ahead ✓ 3 min from Marienplatz
Food Highlight
The Weisswurst (white sausage) breakfast is a Munich tradition — served before noon with sweet mustard and a Weissbier. The pork knuckle and crown meat (Kronfleisch) are also worth ordering.
Best for: Anyone who wants to drink their way through Munich's six breweries — this covers Schneider Weisse and gives you a genuinely different beer experience from the other halls.
Suggested Night Plan
A rough itinerary for each night. Adapt freely — you're 19 lads in Munich, not a guided tour.
🍺 Friday Night — The Classic Munich Crawl
3:00 – 5:00 PM
Augustiner-Keller Beer Garden
Arrive, claim tables, first Maß from the wooden barrel. Listen for the bell when a new barrel is tapped. 5 min walk from the Hauptbahnhof — easy first stop off the plane.
5:30 – 8:00 PM
Hofbräuhaus
Head into the Schwemme (main hall). The brass band will be in full swing. Communal tables — you will meet strangers. They will become your friends. Order Maß, eat Schweinshaxe, make noise. Book a table in advance for the group.
8:30 – 11:00 PM
Tegernseer Tal
5-minute walk from Hofbräuhaus. Continue into the spectacular glass-roofed hall. A different beer (Tegernseer vs Hofbräu) — do the comparison. Late-closing beer hall means no rush.
11:00 PM – 2:00 AM
Kilian's Irish Pub
Full band on stage. Cold Guinness. Sports on every screen. This is where you end up singing until you lose your voice. Karaoke downstairs if the band stops. Closes 3am Friday.
1:00 AM+
Wherever the night takes you
Munich has late-night bars scattered through the Glockenbach and Altstadt areas. Follow the locals, find a bar with the lights still on, and count your Maß.
☀️ Saturday — The Long Day
Midday – 3:00 PM
Viktualienmarkt Beer Garden
Recover with a Maß and market snacks. Self-service, no booking needed, right in the old town. Rotating local brewery on tap. Perfect hangover management.
3:00 – 7:00 PM
Hirschgarten
The world's largest beer garden on a summer afternoon — 8,000 seats, brass band if it's a Sunday, World Cup on 5 big screens. Take the S-Bahn to Hirschgarten stop. This is the money session of the weekend.
7:30 – 10:00 PM
Augustiner Stammhaus
The locals' choice — less tourist, more character. Try the Augustiner Edelstoff in the original Stammhaus setting. Vaulted ceilings, candlelight, proper Munich atmosphere.
10:00 PM – close
Löwenbräukeller or Kilian's
Löwenbräukeller closes midnight and is a spectacular send-off — live music, proper Löwenbräu beer in a grand hall. Or head back to Kilian's for the band and a nightcap. Either way, a fitting end to the weekend.
Practical Tips
Things you'll want to know before you drink your way across Munich.
📅
Book Ahead for Groups
For the Hofbräuhaus and Kilian's with 19 people, email or call ahead. Beer halls are more relaxed about walk-ins but weekends in June fill fast. Augustiner-Keller has an online event configurator for group bookings.
⚽
World Cup 2026 is On
The FIFA World Cup runs June 11 – July 19, 2026. Matches will be shown across Munich — beer gardens, Kilian's, and the Hirschgarten's big screens. This will make the city even more electric. Check match schedules for your dates.
🍺
The Maß Explained
A Maß is 1 litre. That's your unit of currency in Munich. Budget roughly €10–13 per Maß in beer halls, slightly less in beer gardens. Weissbier (wheat beer) usually comes in 0.5L glasses. Order by pointing and holding up fingers.
🚇
Getting Around
The U-Bahn and S-Bahn run until around 1:30am, then night buses every 30 minutes. Taxis and Uber are plentiful after that. The Hirschgarten is S-Bahn Hirschgarten stop. Most beer halls are walkable from Marienplatz.
🌾
Helles vs Weissbier vs Dunkel
Munich's three main styles: Helles (pale lager, crisp and easy), Weissbier/Weiss (wheat beer, fruity and cloudy — the local favourite), and Dunkel (dark lager, malty and smooth). Try all three over the weekend. Schneider Aventinus is the wildcard — a dark wheat doppelbock at 8.2% that drinks dangerously easily.
🥨
Food Strategy
Beer halls serve food late (Hofbräuhaus kitchen to 10pm). The Schweinshaxe (pork knuckle), Brez'n (pretzels), and Wurstsalat are essential. In beer gardens, self-service counters sell grilled food. Hirschgarten's Steckerlfisch (grilled fish) is legendary.
💰
Budget Guide
Beer halls: €10–13/Maß. Beer gardens: €9–12/Maß. Irish pub pints: €7–9. Club entry: €10–20. Club drinks: €12–18. A solid night in beer halls costs about €50–70 all in. Clubs add another €50–80. Munich is cheaper than London for the quality on offer.
🎺
Oompah Etiquette
When the brass band plays at the Hofbräuhaus, everyone bangs their stein on the table in time. You are not exempt. Learn "Prost!" (cheers), "Noch ein Bier bitte" (another beer please), and "Wo ist die Toilette?" (where is the toilet). These three phrases will carry you through.
🌧
June Weather Plan B
June in Munich is usually warm (18–25°C) but afternoon thunderstorms aren't unheard of. Augustiner-Keller has a fantastic underground cellar that opens if the beer garden closes due to weather. All beer halls are indoors. You will not go thirsty.
